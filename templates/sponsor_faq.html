Sponsor FAQ

Mentors

Our goal with HackIllinois is to empower students to experiment with their ideas, and to make a space where it’s encouraged to try something new. We want everyone to leave on Sunday proud of what they built. As sponsors, you have incredibly smart engineers working on all aspects of technology and can bring that expertise to HackIllinois. The most successful hackathon mentors make themselves available to help with any problem a team runs into, and that’s the sort of collaboration we’re working to encourage.

We’ll provide more specifics on mentoring as we get closer to the event, but we're mentioning it now so you can find the best engineers for the job. 36 hours is a long time, and we encourage you to pick people who are energetic!

Recruiters

Hackathons are an awesome opportunity to see the best students at work. One of our sponsors told us “one thing we’ve learned from hackathons is that, unlike a career fair, sponsors are sort of secondary. They come to help hackathon attendees achieve their goals, and in the end a hacker will remember the logo on the jacket of the person that helped them.” Recruiters come to hackathons to build face-to-face relationships with students, and you can see what a team is creating as they’re building it. There won’t be a standard career fair, but there’s a place for you to meet teams in person. We’re also providing resumes and other candidate information online.

Tech Talks

If your sponsorship includes a tech talk, it will happen Saturday April 12th for up to 60 minutes. We will be scheduling these soon. Let us know if you have a preference for a specific time (or if you’d rather go Friday night), and we will do our best to accommodate you.


We’ve learned from other hackathons, and the best talks seem to be one or more of the following.

  • Tutorial: Hold an early session where you help get hackers set up with your product, or offer general help with something like iOS or Android. Hackers like to work on new things, and Friday night is a perfect time for them to learn.
  • API Demos: Show hackers how your API can save them time or allow them to do something cool. (Bonus points if you enable a student to do something they didn’t know they could do!)
  • The unexpected or inspirational: Surprise us, and attendees, with what you do with your session. Bring something fun from HQ or some technology we don’t see every day!
  • Interesting Engineers: If you have an employee who is influential in the tech community or has done something great, try to bring them.
  • Raffles & Prizes: Hackers LOVE free stuff and a great way to boost attendance is to offer raffles or prizes during your tech talk. Be creative with what you bring!


We’ve also learned some things that do not work:

  • Recruiting Sessions: This is not your average weekday afternoon! Many students do want a job, but for these 36 hours their concern is creating their projects.
  • Systems Explanations: Students are interested in how you’ve innovated in technology, but this weekend is an intense period of building for them. Try to align your talk with what they’re looking for.
